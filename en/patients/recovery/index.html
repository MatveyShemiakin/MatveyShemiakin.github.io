First days after surgery

Why vision may still be blurred and which symptoms are urgent

How to distinguish expected recovery after cataract surgery from warning signs that should not wait for a routine appointment.

Symptoms vary between patients. The trend matters: during expected recovery, discomfort should lessen and vision should gradually improve.

On this pageWhy is my vision not perfect?Which symptoms are expected?Which symptoms need urgent care?

Why is my vision not perfect immediately after surgery?

Short answer. Blurred or fluctuating vision in the first hours or days is common and does not by itself mean that a complication has occurred.

Vision may be affected by a dilated pupil, corneal swelling, mild inflammation, drops or ointment, a dry ocular surface and a temporary difference between the two eyes.

Some patients notice improvement the next day, while others recover over several days or weeks. The final result also depends on the cornea, retina, optic nerve and conditions such as glaucoma.

Important
  • Look at the trend: vision should gradually improve and discomfort should lessen.
  • Use the prescribed drops exactly as directed.
  • Contact the clinic urgently if vision deteriorates rapidly or improves and then becomes worse.

Which symptoms are expected after cataract surgery?

Short answer. Mild discomfort, a gritty or foreign-body sensation, tearing, slight redness and light sensitivity can occur during the first few days.

You may also notice mild burning, stinging, slight aching, eyelid swelling or brighter perception of colours. Expected symptoms should gradually settle, although recovery may take longer after a more complex procedure.

Important
  • Do not rub the eye or try to remove a perceived speck.
  • Mild improving discomfort may be expected; severe or increasing pain is not.
  • Purulent discharge or worsening redness needs assessment.

Which symptoms require urgent medical attention?

Short answer. Do not wait for a routine appointment if vision drops suddenly, pain becomes severe or increasing, redness worsens, or new flashes, many floaters or a curtain appear.

Urgent assessment is also needed if vision worsens after initially improving, if marked light sensitivity develops, if the eyelids become increasingly swollen or if unusual sticky or purulent discharge appears.

Use the clinic contact number in your discharge information. If the clinic cannot be reached, seek urgent ophthalmic care.

Do not
  • wait several days hoping the symptoms will settle;
  • rely only on social media messages;
  • alter treatment yourself;
  • drive if you have sudden severe visual loss or pain.
